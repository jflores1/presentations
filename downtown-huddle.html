E-Commerce isn't going anywhere.

4 Key Concepts for E-Commerce Success

  1. Customer Journies: Wandering, but not lost
  2. Go where the customers are. Then, bring 'em home.
  3. "Brick & Mortar" "Click & Mortar"
  4. Reward good behaviors
- I won't have time to go into a lot of detail - I'll focus on restaurants - But this is a general abstraction - Applies to 'local' and not local businesses

A day in the life (Pre-Covid)...

  • Get up, go to the gym
  • Wrap up workout, order Tropical Smoothie & Panera via app
  • Pick them up, head to the office
  • Lunch (meeting) at Panera (kiosk, table, app)
- Begin with the end in mind - Transactions were _so easy_ that I almost _couldn't NOT_ do it

This is where you want to be...

In someone's routine.

- Routine doesn't mean daily - Just habitual

So, how do we orchestrate this?

By adapting to customer buying behavior...

What was the last think you bought online?

How does this work?

  • A customer has a need/want
  • Thinks about how to get it fulfilled
  • Searches for answers, usually on a mobile device
  • Decides, acts, evaluates & shares (hopefully)
- Begin with the end in mind - Transactions were _so easy_ that I almost _couldn't NOT_ do it

(Some) Typical behavior includes...

  • Searching Google
  • Searching aggregators (Yelp, Doordash, etc)
  • Reading Reviews
  • Validating reviews
  • Checking out websites
  • Asking friends/family for recommendations (Facebook)
  • Determining whether a transaction can take place without getting off the couch

This underscores 2 key points:

  1. Customer journies aren't one-way events
  2. Multichannel is a must

You want to reduce friction as much as possible to remain in the "Initial Set" and close the inner loop.

The key to in-store will be (as it was before)...

Worthwhile uniqueness

(Preferably experiential)

- Why I love Panera - En Vie for nice dinner

Key Takeaway:

Make the in-store experience worthwhile

Final Takeaway:

Keep 'em coming back

I discovered the Panera deal in-store

But the rewards keep/kept me committed

(Ditto: Starbucks)

Start a rewards program

  • Gamify it
  • Make it easy to sign up
  • Give the first reward quickly
  • And the second...
  • Then, start to space them out.
  • Use it to encourage sharing, too
- You want to form a habit - Small quick wins, eventually harder to get

How to put this all together?

  • Start with channel presence
  • Get a website that can integrate with your services
  • Focus on the whole journey, then build backwards

Recommendation: Start an experience journey journal

Example:

  • Wordpress/WooCommerce Website
  • Woo -> Square Integration
  • Square -> DoorDash

Outcomes: one system, update everywhere with minimal touches.

- Woo: Square menu + payment processing - Square for processing Cannabis, also rewards & POS - Square to Doordash coming soon - Challenge: integrating with email marketing - Still: market leads in software are always worth investing in
